Download the Extension Files:

Ensure you have the extension files ready on your local machine. This typically includes files like manifest.json, popup.html, popup.js, content.js, and any associated CSS or image files.
Open Chrome Browser:

Launch the Google Chrome browser on your computer.
Access Chrome Extensions Page:

Type chrome://extensions/ in the address bar and press Enter. This will take you to the Extensions management page.
Enable Developer Mode:

Toggle the Developer mode switch located at the top right corner of the Extensions page. This will allow you to load unpacked extensions.
Load the Extension:

Click on the Load unpacked button.
Navigate to the directory where your extension files are stored, select the folder, and click Open. The extension will now be added to your Chrome browser.
Verify Installation:

Once loaded, the extension should appear in the list of installed extensions on the Extensions page. You can check if it is active and view any relevant details.
How to Unload (Remove) a Chrome Extension
Open Chrome Browser:

Access Chrome Extensions Page:

Type chrome://extensions/ in the address bar and press Enter.
Locate the Extension:

Scroll through the list of installed extensions to find the one you wish to remove.
Remove the Extension:

Click the Remove button (typically shown as a trash can icon) next to the extension you want to uninstall.
A confirmation dialog will appear asking if you want to remove the extension. Click Remove to confirm.
Verify Removal:

The extension should disappear from the list, indicating that it has been successfully removed from Chrome.
Optional: Disable Instead of Removing
If you wish to temporarily stop the extension without uninstalling it:

On the Extensions page, toggle the switch next to the extension to the off position. This will disable the extension while keeping it installed.
